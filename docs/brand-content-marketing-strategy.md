# Brand, Content & Marketing Strategy

## wireservers.com — Services Authority

**Site type:** Technical blog, case studies, portfolio

**Content:**
- Deep architecture posts — "How we built a diet intelligence API", "Scaling React Native across 5 apps with a shared component library"
- Case studies featuring BringThe apps as flagship products
- Consulting/services landing pages
- "Built with react-natives" showcase

**Marketing:**
- SEO on technical/consulting keywords
- Cross-post to Dev.to and LinkedIn
- Every post subtly demonstrates capability by referencing your own products

**Relationship to others:** The "by WireServers" badge on every BringThe app and reactnatives.dev. Consulting leads come from people who see what you've built and want the same.

---

## BringThe___.com — Consumer Products

Each app gets its own domain and should feel like a standalone product, not a sub-brand.

**Content per app:** Problem-specific SEO content that doubles as product demos.
- **Diet:** "Is oat milk AIP compliant?", "Hidden dairy ingredients to watch for", "7-day FODMAP meal plan"
- **Events:** "Wedding planning timeline checklist", "Corporate event checklist"
- **List, Games, Projects:** Same pattern — SEO-driven content specific to the problem domain, funnel to the app

**Marketing:**
- Content SEO is the primary channel — people with restrictive diets, event planning stress, etc. are actively searching
- Long-tail keywords with high intent and low competition
- Social media for recipes/tips/templates that drive back to the app
- Diet-specific landing pages (one per restriction: celiac, keto, halal, AIP, FODMAP, etc.) are SEO goldmines
- Comparison pages: "BringTheDiet vs MyFitnessPal for restrictive diets"

**Cross-linking between apps:**
- Contextual only. "Planning food for guests with allergies?" links diet to events
- Shared footer: "Part of the BringThe family" with sibling links
- Each app stands alone but the family grows perceived value

**Relationship to wireservers.com:** "by WireServers" in the footer. Portfolio entries on wireservers.com. The BringThe apps are your best case studies.

---

## reactnatives.dev — Developer Tools

**Site type:** Docs + component showcase

**Content:**
- Interactive component playground/storybook
- Clean docs, copy-paste examples
- Installation/usage guides
- "Built with react-natives" gallery showing BringThe apps as real-world usage
- Comparison content: "react-natives vs NativeBase vs Tamagui"

**Marketing:**
- npm/GitHub presence first
- Comparison posts published on the wireservers.com blog and cross-posted to Dev.to
- Engage in React Native communities on X, Discord, Reddit

**Relationship to others:** wireservers.com blog hosts the deep technical content about the library. BringThe apps are the proof it works in production — "70+ components, used across 5 shipping apps." Developers who adopt the library discover WireServers consulting.

## The Flywheel

```
reactnatives.dev ──> devs discover ──> wireservers.com (consulting leads)
        ^                                        |
   powers the UI                          "we built these"
        |                                        v
BringThe apps <──── content SEO traffic <── consumer demand
```

Each brand feeds the others:
- **BringThe** drives consumer traffic and proves the stack
- **react-natives** earns developer trust and validates the components
- **wireservers.com** converts both audiences into consulting revenue

---

## Marketing Priority Order

1. **BringTheDiet content SEO** — highest volume, lowest cost. Restrictive diet content has real search demand and low competition compared to generic fitness. Every piece of content is also a product demo.
2. **reactnatives.dev community building** — slow burn but compounds. Each new component/star is a touchpoint.
3. **wireservers.com blog** — technical content that supports both other brands, plus drives consulting leads.

**Key insight:** BringTheDiet is doing double duty — it's a consumer product and a showcase for your component library. Prioritize it as your content engine and let the other brands draft behind it.
